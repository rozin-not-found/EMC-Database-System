# Event Management Customizer (EMC) Database System

## Overview
This repository contains the relational database design and implementation for the Event Management Customizer (EMC). EMC is a start-up centre based in Subang Jaya, Malaysia that specializes in booking adventure-filled holidays and team-building events. This system allows EMC to effectively store, manage, and query information regarding their diverse clientele, accommodation options, and facility hires.

## Project Scope & Business Logic
The database is engineered to handle several core business operations:
* **Client Management:** Records detailed information for various client types, including families, businesses, governmental institutions, and schools.
* **Accommodation Tracking:** Manages bookings across different accommodation types, including a four-star hotel (Snoopy Lupiz), large hostel dormitories, and camping grounds.
* **Facility Allocation:** Tracks the hiring of indoor facilities (board meeting rooms, conference halls) and outdoor pursuits (climbing walls, paintball, canoes).
* **Staff Assignment:** Assigns experienced staff members to facilitate specific team-building activities and tracks their sales performance.
* **Financial Transactions:** Manages base fares, dynamic hire charges, and payment tracking for all complete bookings.

## Database Schema
The database consists of the following core tables structured to maintain referential integrity:
* `Client`: Stores organizational and representative details.
* `Accommodation` & `Room`: Handles accommodation inventory and geographical location data.
* `Facilities`: Catalogs all indoor/outdoor amenities and associated costs.
* `Staff`: Records employee departments, employment status, and contact info.
* `Booking`, `BookingAccommodation`, & `BookingFacility`: The central transaction tables linking clients to their chosen dates, rooms, and activities.
* `Payment`: Manages total transaction amounts and payment methods.

## Analytical Reporting
The SQL script (`Complete_EMS_Code.sql`) contains complex queries designed to extract meaningful business intelligence, including:
* Calculating the average duration of stays across different accommodation types.
* Identifying top-tier clients based on total transaction amounts.
* Analyzing the total revenue generated by individual staff members.
* Projecting raw income adjustments based on dynamic price increases (e.g., a 6% hike in booking charges).
